Alternatives to Predatory Banking
not-for-profit

Credit Unions

Member-owned, not shareholder-owned. Lower fees, higher savings rates, actual humans who know your name. NCUA-insured same as banks.

Find yours → mycreditunion.gov
zero fees

Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs)

Banks chartered specifically to serve low-income communities. More flexible loan criteria. Many offer Individual Development Accounts (IDAs) that match your savings 2:1 or 3:1.

Find CDFIs → cdfifund.gov
mutual

Lending Circles

A group of people who each contribute monthly to a pool, and each member gets the full pool once. Zero interest. Builds credit. Used for centuries across cultures (tandas, susus, chit funds).

Mission Asset Fund model →
free account

Chime / Current / Dave

No monthly fees, no minimum balance, no overdraft fees ($34 average at traditional banks). Not perfect, but better than being unbanked.

chime.com

Bitcoin / Lightning

Send money anywhere, to anyone, for near-zero cost. No bank permission needed. Receive international payments in seconds. Not investment advice — a tool for financial access.

Buy-Nothing / Mutual Aid

The oldest bank: people sharing what they have. Buy Nothing groups (Facebook, app), Freecycle, and local mutual aid networks. See the SolarPunk Mutual Aid board.

Free or Low-Cost Loans

Kiva

0% interest microloans up to $15,000 for entrepreneurs. Funded by a global crowd. US residents qualify. No credit score required.

kiva.org/borrow

SBA Microloans

Up to $50,000 for small businesses. 6.5% interest max (better than any credit card). Flexible terms, no collateral for small amounts.

sba.gov/loans

USDA Farm Loans

Microloans for farmers and ranchers. Up to $50,000. Flexible repayment. Specifically designed for beginning and underserved farmers.

fsa.usda.gov

Emergency Rental Assistance

Many states still have ERA funds available for people behind on rent. Search: "[your state] emergency rental assistance 2024"

treasury.gov ERA →
