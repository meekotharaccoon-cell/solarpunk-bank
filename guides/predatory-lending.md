# Predatory Lending: What It Is and How to Fight It

## What Is Predatory Lending

Predatory lending uses deceptive, unfair, or abusive loan terms to extract maximum money from borrowers. It targets people with fewer options — low income, bad credit, urgent need.

Common forms:
- **Payday loans:** 400%+ APR, trap borrowers in rollover cycles
- **Rent-to-own:** Often 100-200% effective APR on furniture and electronics
- **Car title loans:** High APR + risk of losing your car
- **Buy Here Pay Here dealers:** Inflated prices, high interest, GPS to repo remotely
- **Check cashing fees:** 1-5% per check when a bank account would cost $0

---

## Your Rights

### Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
Lenders must disclose the APR before you sign. If they don't, that's a TILA violation. File at: **cfpb.gov/complaint**

### Military Lending Act
If you're active duty military (or dependent): 36% APR cap on all consumer loans. Lenders who violate this face criminal penalties. File at: **consumer.ftc.gov**

### State laws
Many states have APR caps (Colorado: 36%, California: 36%, Illinois: 36%). If a lender charges more than your state cap, that's illegal.
Search: "[state] payday loan law APR cap"

## How to Report Predatory Lenders

1. **CFPB:** cfpb.gov/complaint — most powerful federal enforcement agency
2. **FTC:** ftc.gov/complaint
3. **State AG:** search "[state] attorney general consumer complaint"
4. **State banking regulator:** search "[state] department of financial institutions"

Your complaint creates a public record. It helps regulators build cases. It helps others find the truth about the lender. File even if you don't expect individual resolution.

## If You're Already Trapped

### Payday loan rollover cycle
1. Contact the lender in writing: request an Extended Payment Plan (EPP). Many states require them to offer it.
2. Contact your state AG’s office — they often have payday loan mediation programs
3. Consider bankruptcy consultation (free consultations at most firms) — payday debt is dischargeable

### Car title loan
1. Know your state's repossession law — some states require notice before repo
2. Some states let you redeem the title after repossession
3. If you believe the terms were misrepresented: file CFPB complaint immediately

### Unmanageable debt of any kind
1. **National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC):** nfcc.org — free or low-cost counseling
2. **Debt management plans:** not a loan, not bankruptcy, a negotiated repayment schedule
3. **Bankruptcy:** Chapter 7 discharges most unsecured debt in 3-4 months. Filing fee: $338. Many bankruptcy attorneys offer payment plans.
